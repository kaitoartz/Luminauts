What makes a good error message?
This is part five of my series of building with good UX.
In the last three parts, we went deep on loading states,
and now we're going to error states.
And if you're building with AI tools,
unless you specifically ask for it,
most tools are pretty bad at creating error states
by default.
First is your error message should never be
your database or backend logic just dumped on the screen.

One, being how would a regular person
even know what that means?
And two, this could also be a security vulnerability
if you're exposing details about your app's backend.
Okay, so then you just put something went wrong, right?
wrong. Your user doesn't need to know the technical details, but they do need something
less vague than that. Think about it. If you enter your card information and you press pay
and it says something went wrong, you have no idea if your payment went through or not. That
is a pretty terrible user experience. A good error message does three things. It tells them what
happened, why it happened, and gives them a clear action. So instead of saying something went wrong,

you say your payment didn't go through, your card was declined. Please check your card details or
try a different payment method. Now the user knows exactly what happened, why, and what's next. And
And then there's the worst kind of error, the silent failure.
No message at all.
The user clicks submit, the button does nothing, the screen doesn't change.
They don't know if it worked, if it broke.
And this is what a lot of vibe coding AI tools generate by default.
So you need to know these things to know what to ask for.
